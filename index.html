Sites to see

One must see site is the Old Cathedral of Cuenca. It was built in 1557 and was the main place of worship during the Spanish colonization. When you walk inside the church one thing that you will notice is how quiet it is inside. You can literally hear footsteps against the floor.

Food scene

In Cuenca there all kinds of food options but one of my favorite treats was having a fresh plate of figs with cheese. It may sound like an odd combination but I promise, the taste is out of this world. You can also find Humitas, which are muffins that are made with ground corn, tortillas de morocho which is very similar to a pancake and made with ground corn, and locro de papa which is a creamy potato soup.

Things to do

One of my favorite activities is to pass through a town called Chordeleg. This town is known for it's silver and jewelery stores. You can also find other businesses such as artisan shops, clothing stores, and other gift shops.

About

Overall Cuenca is a great place to travel to if you are looking for a place to visit where most of the attractions are within walking distance or a short bus/car ride away. The weather is on the cooler side but good year round, typically between 40 to 70 degrees depending on the season. As it is a small city, there is definitely a strong sense of community and people generally really open and friendly.
